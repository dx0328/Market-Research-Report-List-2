<p><p>A microcarrier is a small spherical particle made from materials such as polystyrene or dextran that is used in cell culture to support the growth of anchorage-dependent cells. <p><p>Microcarrier market types include cationic microcarriers, which are positively charged and suitable for cell attachment and transfection. Collagen-coated microcarriers promote cell adhesion and proliferation, while protein-coated microcarriers enhance cell growth and function. Untreated microcarriers serve as a basic option for cell culture applications. These diverse microcarrier options cater to a range of cell culture requirements and applications.</p></p>
