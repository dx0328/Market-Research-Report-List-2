<p><p>Dialysis Catheters are medical devices used to access the bloodstream for hemodialysis in patients with kidney failure. The market growth is being driven by the increasing prevalence of chronic kidney diseases, rising geriatric population, and the growing number of patients requiring dialysis globally. Additionally, advancements in catheter technology, such as the development of antimicrobial catheters to reduce the risk of infection, are also contributing to market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the Dialysis Catheters Market is the adoption of tunneled catheters, which are designed to reduce the risk of infection and provide greater stability. These catheters are implanted under the skin and tunnelled to the vein, making them less prone to displacement or infection. The trend towards minimally invasive procedures and the use of advanced materials in catheter manufacturing are also driving market growth.</p></p>

<p><p>Dialysis catheters are categorized into two main types based on their usage duration: long-term hemodialysis catheters and short-term hemodialysis catheters. Long-term catheters are designed for extended use, often for several weeks to months, while short-term catheters are intended for temporary use, typically for up to a few weeks. Each type serves the purpose of providing access for dialysis treatment, with long-term catheters offering durability and reliability for extended periods, and short-term catheters being more suitable for temporary dialysis needs.</p></p>
