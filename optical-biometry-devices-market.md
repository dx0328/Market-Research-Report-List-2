<p><p>Optical biometry devices are used for measuring various parameters of the eye, such as axial length, corneal thickness, and intraocular lens power calculation. There are two main types of optical biometry devices: contact-type and non-contact type. Contact-type devices require physical contact with the eye, whereas non-contact type devices do not require direct contact with the eye. Both types have their own advantages and disadvantages, and the choice between them depends on factors such as patient comfort, accuracy, and ease of use.</p></p>
